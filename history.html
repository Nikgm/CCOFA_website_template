Our History

A Legacy Established in 1956

In the summer of 1956, a small but determined group of Florinians gathered in the offices of Ilias Mihailidis in Melbourne. They were migrants far from home but united by something powerful: a deep love for Florina and an unwavering desire to preserve their identity in a new land.

From that meeting came a defining decision: to establish a cultural centre for Florinians in Melbourne. They chose the name "Aristotelis", synonymous with the respected club in Florina itself, as a symbol of continuity, pride and connection to homeland.

Following that historic meeting in 1956, the first committee was formed:

  • President: Iraklis Kapoulitsas
  • Vice President: Antonis Koraitsas
  • Secretary: Aristotelis Pavlidis
  • Treasurer: Anastasios Dimtsis
  • Committee Members: Ilias Mihailidis and Dimkos Karavasilis

With unity and passion, this founding committee organised Aristotelis's first dance, followed by a community picnic. These were more than social gatherings. They were lifelines. Through music, dance, food, laughter and shared stories, they recreated the spirit of Florina in Melbourne and bridged the emotional distance between homeland and new home.

Over the decades, Aristotelis grew alongside its members. Children who once watched rehearsals from the sidelines became dancers. Dancers became teachers. Teachers became leaders. Families formed lifelong friendships under the banner of Aristotelis, united by a shared love for their heritage and community.

For seventy years, our purpose has remained constant: to ensure our culture is not only preserved, but actively lived and shared.

Today, that founding vision continues to guide us. The Cultural Centre of Florinians "Aristotelis" is more than a community organisation; it is a living bridge between generations. It is a place where traditions are practised, not just remembered; where identity is strengthened, friendships are formed, and the culture of Florina continues to thrive with pride in Melbourne.
